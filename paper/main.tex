\begin{abstract}
We study how neural networks and exact kernel methods combine in operator
learning: an accurate neural mean, trained in the metric the application
reports, with an exact Mat\'ern solve applied to its residual or to its
learned features. Two public benchmarks with published baselines sit in
opposite regimes and the same components explain both. On the
structural-mechanics problem of de Hoop et al.\ the pipeline reaches
\errBest\% relative test error, matching the best published result, and the
reason no method moves much further is measurable: the residuals of every
architecture we train correlate above 0.86, and their shared component
concentrates where the finite element data are least accurate and responds
neither to capacity, diversity, nor sample size. On the OCO-2
radiative-transfer emulation problem of Lamminp\"a\"a et al.\ the same kernel
that trails the network tenfold on the raw input overtakes it on the
network's learned features, and the resulting surrogate beats the published
kernel-flow emulator on its own test points on both of that problem's
metrics (3.8\% against 16.9\% on reduced radiances, 0.027\% against 0.045\%
on the reconstructed spectrum). Each stage of the pipeline carries a
supporting result, from a second-moment identity that predicts the stacking
outcome from measured residual correlations, through an optimal-recovery
bound and rates separating the metric from the representational part of a
network's advantage over a fixed kernel, to a distribution-free coverage
guarantee for the reported uncertainty, which is the only uncertainty signal
that survives our tests.
\end{abstract}
